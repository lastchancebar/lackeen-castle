About Lackeen Castle......

Lackeen Castle, in North Tipperary, was built by the O'Kennedys in the 11th century, about the time the Normans invaded Ireland. The O'Kennedy clan trace their roots to Brian Ború, High King of Ireland, and are ancestors of one of America's best-known families. Kennedys are famous in arts and sport in Ireland and the UK.


The castle, a tower house, has some interesting features built in to deter invaders. Click here to find out how the O' Kennedys dealt with unwelcome visitors.


A priceless manuscript - the Stowe Missal -was found hidden in the castle walls 300 years ago. The book was written nearby over 1,000 years ago, and has a beautifully ornate storage box or reliquary.
