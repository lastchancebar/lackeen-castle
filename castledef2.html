Defending Lackeen

Ireland was a dangerous place 1,000 years ago. Defence was key in castle design to protect the owner from unfriendly clans and Norman invaders. Here are some typical medieval defence features to be seen in Lackeen Castle.

Machicolations

Defenders threw rocks, hot sand, tar and boiling liquids on the invaders below from these holes in the floor of the castle rooftop.

Narrow Windows

Defenders shot arrows and charges from inside at invaders, with slim chance of getting hit in return.

Murder Hole

Defenders hid in a hole above the door to attack invaders who bypassed the arrows, rocks and boiling liquids. Did the ghost of Lackeen die here....?
