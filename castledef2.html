defending Lackeen

Ireland was a dangerous place 1,000 years ago. Defence was key in castle design to protect the clan from unfriendly clans and Norman invaders. Here are some defence features in Lackeen Castle.

Murder Hole

Defenders hid in wait in a hole above the door to attack invaders who bypassed the arrows, rocks and boiling liquids.

Machicolations

Defenders threw rocks, hot sand, tar and boiling liquids on the invaders below from these holes in the floor of the castle rooftop.

Narrow Windows

For shooting arrows and bullets at invaders without getting hit in return.
