The Stowe Missal

Manuscript of national importance

About the Stowe Missal ......

This diminutive hand-written prayer book, in its ornate storage box, was found in the walls of Lackeen Castle 300 years ago. The 1,000 year old book must have been hidden for hundereds of years.

The 15 cm tall book, written in nearby Lorrha, contains the text of the Latin Mass, Sacrament prayers, St John's Gospel, and as an added extra, three healing charms in Gaelic, one for the prick of a thorn!


The Stowe Missal is in the Royal Irish Academy in Dublin.

First page of the Stowe Missal manuscript.

The storage box or reliquary, older than the book it contains, is a stunning piece of Celtic crafsmanship and has the names of several important chieftains and monks engraved in the precious metals, which gives us clues as to its history.

Front of the Stowe Missal reliquary or storage box.

And the final ancient handwritten words in this precious book is our message to all who come this way......



Final line of the Stowe Missal, written in Gaelic and Ogham, wishing the reader good health.
